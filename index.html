Problem

In home warranty and insurance, claim resolution speed is the single biggest driver of customer satisfaction — and the biggest operational cost. Liberty Home Guard faced three interconnected challenges that were limiting growth, increasing costs, and creating friction for customers:

1. Claim Processing Bottleneck: Every incoming claim required a live agent to manually diagnose the issue, coordinate with technicians, and negotiate service pricing. Long wait times frustrated homeowners, per-claim costs were high, and the process couldn't scale without hiring more agents.

2. Technician Onboarding & Verification: Verifying each technician's identity, trade license, Certificate of Insurance (COI), and Driver's License was 100% manual — a human reviewer checked every document individually. This caused onboarding delays, created undetectable identity fraud risk, and couldn't handle volume spikes.

3. Business Intelligence Gap: Business users and executives had no self-service access to operational data. Understanding claim volumes, technician performance, regional bottlenecks, or cost trends required filing an engineering ticket — slowing down decision-making and preventing proactive problem identification across the business.

Solution — 3 Production AI Systems

Project 1 — Voice AI Agent (Claim Intake, Resolution & Technician Negotiation)

Architected and deployed a production Voice AI agent using FastAPI as the backend, ElevenLabs for real-time voice synthesis, and AWS for infrastructure. The agent handles the Technician Diagnosis collection in the claim lifecycle: it conducts a structured diagnostic conversation with Technicians to identify the issue (appliance failure type, severity, history), cross-references coverage terms to determine valid resolutions, and then engages directly with technicians in real-time to negotiate service rates within approved cost bands — completely eliminating the need for a live agent on every call. Deployed as a containerized microservice on AWS ECS/ECR with App Runner for auto-scaling and ALB routing traffic across instances.

Project 2 — Technician Onboarding Verification System

Built a zero-manual-intervention identity and document verification pipeline using AWS Rekognition. The system runs a multi-stage verification flow: liveness detection ensures the person is physically present (not a photo or video spoof); face comparison matches the live selfie against the government-issued photo ID; selfie-to-ID matching applies confidence scoring to flag borderline cases. In parallel, automated document extraction parses COI, Trade License, and Driver's License documents — validating expiry dates, coverage amounts, and license categories without any human reviewer involved at any stage. The entire flow completes in seconds.

Project 3 — Databricks Genie RAG Platform

Embedded a natural language business intelligence layer into the internal business portal using Databricks Genie with a custom semantic layer. Business users and executives can now ask questions like "What are the top claim categories in Q1 by region?" or "Which technicians have the highest resolution rates this month?" in plain English and get accurate, data-backed answers instantly. The custom semantic layer maps business terminology directly to underlying data models — ensuring query accuracy without requiring SQL knowledge — and surfaces patterns and bottlenecks that previously required days of engineering effort to uncover.

Problem

The HMS research team was running a large-scale study involving physician interviews across multiple specialties. Each interview was recorded and needed to be transcribed, then analyzed for clinical decision-making patterns, reasoning styles, and thematic insights. Medical audio is notoriously difficult to transcribe accurately due to dense specialized terminology, varying accents, and overlapping speech. Manually transcribing and analyzing hundreds of hours of recordings was taking weeks per analysis cycle — a serious bottleneck for the research timeline and budget.

Solution

Designed and built an end-to-end GenAI pipeline with four layers:

1. Transcription: Used OpenAI Whisper — chosen specifically for its superior accuracy with medical terminology over standard speech-to-text tools — to transcribe physician recordings at scale with high fidelity.

2. Structured Analysis: Applied targeted prompt engineering on GPT to extract structured insights from each transcript: key clinical decision points, reasoning patterns, terminology usage frequency, thematic categories, and cross-physician comparison signals.

3. Pipeline Optimization: Implemented batched async processing and response caching to maximize throughput and minimize API costs — enabling the pipeline to scale across hundreds of recordings without proportional cost increases.

4. Streamlit Research UI: Built a clean, researcher-facing interface to upload recordings, review transcriptions, query AI-generated analysis, and export structured reports — requiring zero technical knowledge to operate independently.

Problem

A separate research track involved capturing physiological signals — ECG, RR intervals, and PPG — from patients during live surgical procedures to study the relationship between heart rate variability (HRV) and surgical outcomes. The existing workflow relied on Kubios, a commercial HRV analysis tool requiring manual, per-participant operation. It couldn't process multiple participants simultaneously, produced no programmatic output for downstream ML analysis, and introduced operator variability that compromised reproducibility. The team needed a scalable, automated, and validated signal analysis workflow.

Solution

Automated HRV Pipeline: Built Python-based ML models using Neurokit2 to programmatically extract and compute HRV metrics from ECG, RR interval, and PPG signals — time-domain, frequency-domain, and non-linear HRV measures — fully automated with no manual steps.

Sliding Window Technique: Implemented a sliding window approach to process continuous physiological signals across varying time windows, enabling consistent HRV metric extraction regardless of recording length or sampling rate variations between participants.

Multi-participant Parallelization: Restructured the pipeline to process multiple participant datasets simultaneously — converting a sequential, manual workflow into a parallelized automated one.

Kubios Benchmarking Study: Ran a rigorous side-by-side comparison of Neurokit2 outputs vs. Kubios on identical datasets using ML comparison models — validating that automated results matched or exceeded manual analysis accuracy, building trust with the research team to fully adopt the new pipeline.
